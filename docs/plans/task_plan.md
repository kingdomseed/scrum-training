# Task Plan — Scrum Training Slide Deck

## Goal
Create a 2-day in-person Scrum training experience for 6-8 people at an art education company ("Duolingo of art"). Deliverable is an HTML slide deck in JHD neobrutalist style with printable facilitator notes and exercise handouts.

## Decisions Made

### Audience & Context
- 6-8 people, non-technical (art education company)
- In-person delivery, Wednesday + Thursday next week
- Jason is a Scrum Master (not licensed PST) — no Scrum.org branding
- Projector for slides, whiteboard available, participants have paper + phones (no laptops)

### Format
- Self-contained HTML slide deck (likely Reveal.js)
- JHD neobrutalist design: Epilogue + Plus Jakarta Sans, #0066ff primary, #ffd700 accent, black borders, hard offset shadows, zero radius
- Separate printable facilitator notes (not built into main app)
- Printable view for handouts
- Original Scrum diagrams (no Scrum.org copyrighted visuals)
- Scrum Guide quotes with CC BY-SA attribution

### Day 1 — "Learn by Building" (Lego4Scrum)
1. **Kickoff** — Introductions, "Characteristics of a Great Team" (real sticky notes on wall), Working Agreements
2. **Fundamentals of Scrum** — Definition, Agile Manifesto & 12 Principles, Cynefin/complexity, Empirical Process (3 pillars)
3. **Exploring the Framework** — Roles (PO, Developers, SM), Events, Artifacts, DoD
4. **Lego Sprint Simulation** — 3 Sprints building art-themed Lego city. Jason plays PO. Physical Scrum board on wall (Product Backlog → Sprint Backlog → Doing → Done)
5. **Day 1 Wrap-up** — Scrum Values discussion, Feedback Wall (sticky notes)

### Day 2 — "Apply to Your Product"
6. **Review & Deeper Dive** — Sprint Events flow, Self-Management, Collaboration & Cross-functionality
7. **Planning with Scrum** — Estimation, Product Backlog Refinement, "Ready" concept, Monitoring Progress
8. **Presentation Sprint Simulation** — 2-3 Sprints where teams build a presentation to sell the vision of their actual product. Day 1 = paper/planning. Day 2 = present.
9. **Getting Started with Scrum** — "Should we use Scrum?" canvas, organizational transformation, identify real PO/SM/Developers for their company
10. **Closing** — Appreciation Wall, suggested reading, next steps

### Lego Exercise
- Product: Lego4Scrum format — build an art-themed Lego city
- Buy: 2x LEGO Classic boxes (Medium 10696 ~$28 or Large 10698 ~$40 each)
- Sprint structure: Planning → Build → Review → Retro
- Jason plays Product Owner

### Presentation Exercise (Day 2)
- Teams build a presentation to sell the vision of their actual product
- Tools: Paper, markers, sticky notes (no laptops)
- Phones OK for reference/photos
- Teams present to the group

### Physical Materials Needed
- 2 LEGO Classic boxes
- Sticky notes (multiple colors)
- Markers
- Large paper / poster boards
- Whiteboard + markers
- Printed facilitator notes
- Printed exercise handouts (Scrum board template, retro formats, etc.)
