## Lego4Scrum Research

Source: `/Users/jholt/Downloads/Lego4Scrum-version2-French.pdf` (CC BY 3.0, Alexey Krivitsky, v2.0 Oct 2011)

### Overview
- Created by Alexey Krivitsky
- Classic product vision: "Build a Lego City" (eco village, art school, etc.)
- One LEGO box per team (minimum 200 pieces)
- Recommended: LEGO Classic Medium (10696, 484pc, ~$28) or Large (10698, 790pc, ~$40)
- For 2 teams: ~$60-80 total
- Facilitator plays Product Owner
- Total game duration: **100-120 minutes**
- Ideal: 2-3 teams of 4-6 people (8-18 total)

### Exact Timings (from the book)

**Pre-game (~50 min):**
| Step | Duration | Running Total |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Team Organization (self-organize into groups, pick tables) | 5 min | 5 min |
| Project Definition (PO pitches vision, collaboration charter) | 10 min | 15 min |
| Build the Backlog (PO presents pre-made sticky notes on paperboard) | 15 min | 30 min |
| Estimation (swimlane sizing 1-2-3-5-8 or planning poker) | 20 min | 50 min |

**Game — 3 Sprints (~45 min):**
| Sprint | Planning | Build | Review | Total |
|--------|----------|-------|--------|-------|
| Sprint 1 | 3 min | 7 min | 5 min | 15 min |
| Sprint 2 | 3 min | 7 min | 5 min | 15 min |
| Sprint 3 | 3 min | 7 min | 5 min | 15 min |

**Post-game (~15 min):**
- Short break after final review (game is deliberately tiring)
- Circle debrief with open questions

### Key Facilitation Notes
- **Sprint 1 is "almost always a catastrophe"** — by design
- PO plays a **beginner PO** who doesn't know much about Scrum/Agile
- At Review, PO asks "Where is my city?" and rejects most Sprint 1 work:
  - "I like symmetry"
  - "Same color = unique color per building, different for each"
  - "Buildings are too small/big, windows not aligned"
- **Use a visible countdown timer** on projector during builds (creates pressure)
- Sprint Planning is only 3 min — teams move stories from Backlog to their Sprint boxes
- Ask teams if they feel "sufficiently *uncomfortable*" with their plan — then start
- Brief retro between sprints: "How can we do better next sprint?"
- After Sprint 2, teams start "continuous deployment" to demo environment
- **3 sprints typically builds ~80% of backlog** at expected quality

### Backlog Items (city-themed, adapt for art school)
Original: single-story buildings, 2-story buildings, shop, school, church, hospital, kindergarten, bus stop, intersection (drawn), public garden (drawn), river (drawn), bridge
- Some items can be drawn on paper with LEGOs placed on top
- Can customize: "silicon village" for startups, etc.

### Planning Wall (physical artifact)
- Backlog on left → Planning Wall grid on right
- Columns: Team A, Team B, Team C
- Rows: Sprint 1, Sprint 2, Sprint 3
- Each cell has: planned points, actual points
- Stories move from Backlog → team's Sprint cell

### Debrief Questions
1. What did you observe?
2. What does it feel like to be on a Scrum team?
3. How do short iterations go?
4. Were estimations accurate?
5. What would you do differently if starting over?
6. What was the PO's job like?
7. How did you feel after Sprint 1 when everything was reworked?
8. What did the Scrum Masters do?
9. How would your strategy change if PO won't be available during Sprints?
10. How was communication within the team? Dependencies?
11. What did you learn?

### Variants
- **Fluctuations**: After Sprint Planning, teams roll a die — multiplies story sizes OR makes team members "sick", forcing adaptation
- Good for teaching that collaboration is essential even when plans change
