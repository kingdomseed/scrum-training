## Slides 1-15: Detailed Findings

### Slide 3 (Introductions)
- Change third prompt to: "Share your name, your role, and one creative project you're proud of"
- Establishes room as creative professionals, gives facilitator callbacks

### Slide 5 (Great Team Characteristics)
- Swap examples to: "a collaborative art project, a department team, a band, a theater production"
- Add 2-min pair-share before wall clustering

### Slide 7 (Origins of Scrum)
- Add on-slide art-education analogy: "Think about how a student art project moves through your department -- is it a relay or a scrum?"
- Currently only in presenter notes

### Slide 8a (What Is Scrum?)
- Discussion question "complex vs complicated" comes BEFORE the slides that define these terms
- Move to after Stacey Matrix, or replace with: "Think of a project where the plan fell apart on contact with reality"

### Slide 8b (Agile Manifesto)
- Replace passive question with structured pair activity: "Turn to your neighbor. Each pick the value that would most transform your department. 60 seconds each."
- Do show-of-hands poll after

### Slide 9 (Stacey Matrix)
- Add visible discussion question (currently buried in notes): "Where does creating a new art curriculum fall on this map?"
- Replace Gantt chart presenter notes with art-education examples

### Slide 10 (Cynefin)
- Add quick classification exercise: classify 3 art-education tasks into domains (3 min)
- Consider whether BOTH Stacey and Cynefin are needed for non-tech audience

### Slide 11a (Empiricism Pillars)
- Add art-critique translations: Transparency = hanging work on wall, Inspection = the critique itself, Adaptation = revising based on feedback
- Add visible discussion question

### Slide 11b (Empiricism Table)
- Consider making it a fill-in exercise instead of pre-filled
- At minimum add show-of-hands: "How many feel strong on Transparency? Inspection? Adaptation?"

### Slide 13 (Framework Overview)
- Add guided storytelling prompt: "Let's trace one Sprint together" with art-education example
- Walk the diagram with a concrete backlog item

### Slides 14-15 (PO, Developers)
- Add art-education role translations on-slide, not just in notes
- S15's "Developers" heading needs immediate reframing for non-tech audience

### Cross-Cutting (S1-15)
- Slides 9-11 are a danger zone: 4 consecutive concept slides with no interaction
- Art-education analogies are mostly in presenter notes -- surface 3-4 on-screen
- No time indicators on exercise slides 3 and 4

## Slides 16-30: Detailed Findings

### Slide 16 (Scrum Master)
- Reframe question: "Think about the last time a student project stalled. What was blocking them -- and whose job was it to remove that block?"
- Add 1-min pair-share before taking answers

### Slide 17 (The Sprint)
- Ground the question: "Imagine your department had to show finished curriculum every two weeks instead of finalizing at semester's end"

### Slide 18 (Sprint Events)
- Add matching exercise: 4 art-education scenarios mapped to Sprint events (2 min)
- Breaks the lecture stretch

### Slide 19 (Artifacts)
- Add bridging analogy: "Product Backlog = wish list, Sprint Backlog = this semester's commitments, Increment = portfolio piece for the faculty exhibition"

### Slide 20 (Definition of Done)
- Keep brief (2 min) -- real DoD learning comes from Lego Sprint 1 rejection
- Add presenter note: "Don't linger here. The simulation will teach this through experience."

### Slide 21 (Lego Divider)
- Change subtitle to signal energy shift: "Time to stand up. You're building an Art School Campus out of Lego."

### Slide 24 (Sprint Rules)
- Add "Definition of Done: TBD -- you'll create this" line
- Plants the seed without prescribing

### Slides 25, 27, 28 (Exercise slides)
- Add visible time allocations on-screen (review report item #2)

### Slide 29 (Lego Debrief)
- Add: "When did you start asking the PO questions? Why not earlier?" (most powerful debrief Q)
- Add: "Think about how your students feel during their first critique. How is that similar?"
- Budget 15-20 minutes minimum; add facilitation note

## Slides 31-45: Detailed Findings

### Slide 31 (Scrum Values)
- Add art-education examples: Commitment = finishing exhibition when kiln breaks; Openness = honest studio critique; Courage = trying a new medium
- Add "think of your own example" moment before S32 exercise

### Slide 34 (Day 1 Closing)
- Add anticipation teaser: "Tomorrow you'll apply everything to YOUR actual product"

### Slide 36 (Day 2 Opening)
- Replace with pair activity: "Explain Scrum to your neighbor in 2 minutes as if they weren't here yesterday"
- Forces recall, surfaces gaps

### Slide 37 (Sprint Events Flow)
- Add art-show analogy: Planning = choose what to exhibit, Daily Scrum = work on pieces, Review = present to audience, Retro = reflect
- Add comprehension check: "Which events did you experience during Lego? Which were missing?"

### Slide 38 (Self-Management)
- Add discussion: "Where in your school do teams already self-manage? Where does someone else decide the WHO, WHAT, or HOW?"
- Gloss "cross-functional": "everyone brings a different skill -- ceramics, digital, administration"

### Slide 40 (Estimation)
- Add 60-second micro-exercise: "How many hours for a student art show? Hard. Is it bigger or smaller than planning a field trip? Much easier."
- Demonstrates relative sizing viscerally

### Slide 41 (Estimation Exercise)
- Provide seed examples: Hang a gallery show, Order supplies, Design a ceramics unit, Write grant for kiln replacement
- Removes blank-page paralysis

### Slide 42 (Refinement)
- Replace "grooming the queue" with: "reviewing your curriculum map mid-semester -- reprioritizing based on what students actually need"

### Slide 43 (Definition of Ready)
- Reframe "Testable" as: "You can describe what 'finished' looks like -- like a rubric for a student project"

### Slide 44 (Monitoring Progress)
- Show a simple burndown chart visual or drop the mention
- Add: "Like checking student progress mid-project -- not to grade, but to see if anyone is stuck"
- Add discussion: "How do you make progress visible in your classroom?"
